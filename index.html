About Prateek Suri

Prateek Suri is not just an entrepreneur—practically a myth-teller of innovation, a legacy builder, and the author of Gateway to Africa, a memoir depicting a journey few dare challenge.

Born in Delhi, India, Prateek's interest in ideas that change lives started early. A Modern School, Barakhamba Road, student, and subsequently a Mechanical Engineering graduate from BITS Pilani, he brought with him a natural affinity for technology and problem-solving. Something that started as curiosity turned into conviction—the conviction that unconventional ideas, pursued with determination, can alter the fate of entire markets.

It was that belief that found expression in Maser Group, the business he created and grew into a $5 billion market-cap giant. With a single focus on democratising tech through affordability and accessibility, he set his sights on Africa—a continent rich in promise but underpenetrated by multinational tech giants. Maser not only retailed more than 800,000 smart TVs but also became a digital divide bridge, empowering families, providing employment, and redefining Africa's tech story. With a successful exit, Prateek initiated MDR Investments with a $500 million fund, venturing into infrastructure, mining, AI, and property—consolidating his position as an economic change catalyst.

But outside the boardroom and the balance sheets is another Prateek: the horseman who hears rhythm and sense in the canter of a horse, the investor who backs courageous start-ups, and the family man who thinks success is not to be measured in terms of dollars, but in terms of lives transformed and strengthened. His philanthropic work—working for African sport, education, and community development—resonates with his conviction that prosperity is not singular.

Prateek's is a tale of courage and persistence, of dream becoming empire, and empire becoming influence. Gateway to Africa is both his personal record and his welcome—to believers, dreamers, and doers everywhere—to pursue the exceptional.

His upcoming book, Gateway to Africa, is more than just a memoir—it serves as a guide for anyone brave enough to pursue opportunities in uncharted territories. Through candid reflections and impactful insights, Prateek shares the lessons, setbacks, and victories that have shaped his evolution from a young dreamer in Delhi to one of Africa’s most influential business figures.

About the Book — Gateway to Africa

Gateway to Africa isn’t just a memoir; it’s like getting a front-row seat to the birth of a billion-dollar dream. In this deeply personal and insightful narrative, Prateek Suri takes us on a journey from the bustling streets of Delhi to the high-stakes boardrooms of Africa, revealing how resilience, risk-taking, and unwavering belief can create empires where it once seemed impossible.

The story kicks off with the poignant loss of his ancestral home in Greater Kailash, a pivotal moment that fueled his determination to reclaim it one day. From his early days at BITS Pilani Dubai to the defining choice to never work for anyone else, Prateek lays the groundwork for a journey driven by ambition and a clear sense of purpose.

As Maser unfolds in these pages, we see how a small electronics trading company transformed into a $5 billion African unicorn, shaking up industries and making technology accessible to all. Each chapter peels back a new layer of the continent—West Africa’s blend of chaos and opportunity, East Africa’s rich cultural tapestry, the untapped mining potential, and the tech revolution that Covid-19 accelerated.

Interspersed throughout are vivid anecdotes that breathe life into Africa: the notorious traffic jams of Lagos, the vibrant Igbo naming ceremonies, evenings spent at Fela Kuti’s shrine, and the lively Swahili chatter in Nairobi’s markets. These aren’t just business lessons; they’re experiences that shaped Prateek’s worldview—you don’t just enter Africa; you’re welcomed in.

Gateway to Africa also casts an eye toward the future, sharing Prateek’s vision for MDR Investments, the burgeoning startup scene in Africa, and the importance of sustainable mining and infrastructure in determining the continent’s future. It’s as much about valuing companies as it is about upholding values, about building not just wealth but also communities and sovereignty.

With honesty and passion, Prateek Suri encourages us to view Africa not as the “market of tomorrow” but as the vibrant heart of today’s global economy. His journey is a testament to the magic that happens when courage meets opportunity.
